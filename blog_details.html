Suicide rate in Kashmir is rising at an alarming pace compared to previous years. In a disturbing trend, the suicide rate has risen sharply last year 2021, with close to 600 people attempting to end their lives in the Kashmir Valley. In Jammu 20 such cases were reported during that period. As the reports of suicides of the year 2022 are yet to come but it looks like it will be much higher than the previous years as we have seen lot of recent suicide cases on the social media (Recently two girls form Baramulla within few days). According to the Crime Gazette 2021 released by Jammu and Kashmir Police, the valley is witnessing an increase in attempted suicide cases with Central Kashmir district Budgam and North Kashmir’s Baramulla toping the tally with 73 cases each. After Budgam and Baramulla, South Kashmir’s Anantnag district witnessed 67 cases while Srinagar reported 51 cases of attempt of suicides. According to a study published in the Indian Journal of Psychiatry, the suicide rate in Kashmir is higher than the national average in India. The study found that the most common reasons for suicide in the region were mental health issues, including depression and anxiety, as well as relationship problems and financial difficulties. The relationship between social media and mental health is extant as social media are being linked to mental health problems like stress, psychological distress, anxiety and depression. In recent times, there is a rising research interest on this topic as research works carried out have established a link between mental health, lifestyle and social media. Social media have been linked to high rates of depression, anxiety and poor sleep, and research has revealed a 70% increase in depression and anxiety resulting from social media use in the past 25 years. Other literatures have also described social media as being more addictive than substances like alcohol and cigarette. There are now such terms as ‘Facebook depression’ which occurs as a result of spending so much time on Facebook and being exposed to the intensity of online activities of others that one starts to exhibit depressive symptoms. FOMO—Fear of Missing Out is another issue of mental health concern that results from the constant use of social media. It is a form of anxiety aroused by the fear that others might be having exciting and rewarding experiences from which one is missing out on. It results in the constant desire to be everywhere events are taking place, the constant need to check one’s phone for the latest updates, the obsessive desire to always stay connected with what others are doing. FOMO often affects both mental and physical health, and it results in the feeling of inferiority, life dissatisfaction, mood swings, low self-esteem, loneliness, increased negative feelings and depression. In the recent suicide case in sopor which was highly glorified by the social media platforms blaming the neighbors and masjid committees, the topic was in trend in the Kashmir valley influencing other persons to have the suicidal thoughts. Lot of people were posting stuffs on social media platforms particularly Facebook that they might end their life if they don’t get any financial support from the people. This effect of social media which intimates the large population to have the suicidal thought is known as the copycat phenomenon. And the suicides which are caused by this phenomenon are called as copycat suicides. The stigma surrounding mental health and seeking help for it can also be a significant barrier to people getting the support they need. Many people may feel ashamed or embarrassed to talk about their feelings of hopelessness or despair, or they may fear being judged or misunderstood by others. There are several warning signs that someone may be considering suicide. These can include: Talking about feeling hopeless, helpless, or worthless, withdrawing from friends and loved ones, Increasing the use of drugs or alcohol, engaging in risky or self-destructive behaviors, changing eating or sleeping habits, Expressing feelings of sadness or despair. It is important to address the root causes of the high rates of suicide in Kashmir and provide support for those who are struggling with mental health issues. This may include increasing access to mental health services, addressing the social and economic factors that contribute to mental health problems. By taking these steps, we can help reduce the number of suicides in Kashmir and improve the overall mental health and well-being of the community. “Nothing in my life has ever made me want to commit suicide more than people’s reaction to my trying to commit suicide.” Emilie Autumn
